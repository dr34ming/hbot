Games

Four games that teach HBOT biology through play. Each one covers different mechanisms—the science sticks better when your hands are involved.

HBOT Tapper

Arcade · Reflexes

Run a hyperbaric facility. Send chambers to patients across four lanes, manage pressurization cycles, and catch returning chambers. Miss a patient and you lose a life. Miss a chamber and it's gone for good.

You learn: The treatment workflow—pressurization, session timing, chamber management. How a real facility operates at scale.

Manage a hyperbaric chamber facility. Send chambers to patients, wait for pressurization, catch them on the return. Gets frantic fast.

You learn: How HBOT sessions work in practice—the equipment, the timing, and why consistency matters.

Pressure Drop

Precision · Timing

Lock a spinning gauge needle into the therapeutic pressure zone (1.4–1.5 ATA). Each successful lock triggers a biological mechanism explanation. Difficulty escalates: direction reversals, dual needles, gauge wobble.

You learn: Dose-response biology. Why 1.5 ATA is the sweet spot. Each lock teaches a specific mechanism—ROS signaling, stem cell mobilization, NF-κB suppression—with real citations.

Time your click to stop a spinning needle in the green zone. Nail the sweet spot and you unlock real biology explanations. Gets harder with direction changes, dual needles, and wobble.

You learn: Why pressure matters, what happens at different levels, and the real science behind each HBOT mechanism.

O₂ Runner

Action · Progression

Pilot a dissolved O2 molecule through the vascular system. Navigate past red blood cells, clots, and narrowing capillaries across five physiological zones. Collect VEGF, HIF-1α, and pressure boost power-ups.

You learn: Oxygen transport physiology. Each zone milestone explains a mechanism: ROS signaling at 500m, stem cell mobilization at 1000m, angiogenesis at 2000m. The power-ups are named after real biology.

You're an oxygen molecule navigating through blood vessels. Dodge obstacles, collect power-ups, and travel deeper into the body. Each zone teaches you something new about how HBOT works.

You learn: How oxygen travels through your body, what it encounters, and the five key mechanisms HBOT activates—delivered as milestone cards as you progress.

Hyperbolic Oxygen

Strategy · Management

Build and manage an HBOT clinic. Buy chambers, set pricing, hire staff, and navigate market dynamics. Patient demand fluctuates with news events and reputation. Satirical take on the wellness industry with real economics underneath.

You learn: The business realities of HBOT—capital costs, pricing elasticity, labor economics, and why home ownership makes financial sense vs. clinic visits over time.

Run your own HBOT clinic. Buy equipment, hire people, set prices, and try to stay profitable while industry events shake things up. A management sim with sharp humor about the wellness world.

You learn: Why HBOT costs what it does, how clinics make money, and the real economics behind home vs. clinic treatment.

Why Games?

Biological mechanisms are easier to internalize through interactive experience than passive reading. Each game maps to different aspects of HBOT science:

  • Pressure Drop teaches dose-response—the relationship between pressure and biological activation thresholds
  • O₂ Runner covers oxygen transport physiology—plasma diffusion, capillary access, and the five major mechanism pathways
  • HBOT Tapper builds intuition for treatment protocols—session flow, frequency, and equipment management
  • Hyperbolic Oxygen grounds the economics—why home ownership outperforms clinic visits at scale

The science content in the games matches the same literature cited on the Science page. Nothing is simplified to the point of being wrong.
